Question Manipulation

schema How Question Bias Is Engineered

The final survey subtly but significantly altered the framing to force positive responses. Use the toggles below to compare.

cleaning_services Cleanliness Question

Carrington's Original
"How would you rate the cleanliness of the store?"

Method: Neutral framing — allows any rating without assumption.

Final (Altered) Version
"How satisfied are you with the store's clean and welcoming environment?"

Bias: Embeds a positive assumption — presumes the store is clean and welcoming.

support_agent Staff Behavior Question

Carrington's Original
"Have you had any negative experiences with store staff?"

Method: Directly invites negative feedback — allows honest reporting of issues.

Final (Altered) Version
"How helpful have staff members been during your visits?"

Bias: Removes the opportunity to report negative encounters — assumes helpfulness.

inventory_2 Product Quality Question

Carrington's Original
"How would you describe the freshness and quality of the produce section?"

Method: Open-ended format — allows both positive and negative descriptions.

Final (Altered) Version
"How satisfied are you with the excellent variety and freshness of our produce?"

Bias: Double-barreled question with built-in positive descriptors ("excellent variety and freshness").

star_rate Overall Experience Question

Carrington's Original
"On a scale of 1–10, how would you rate your overall shopping experience today?"

Method: Full numeric scale — captures the complete range of sentiment.

Final (Altered) Version
"Would you say your experience today was 'Good', 'Very Good', or 'Excellent'?"

Bias: Truncated scale with no neutral or negative options — forces a positive response regardless of actual experience.

error Key Bias Techniques Identified

Leading Language

Embedding positive assumptions directly into the question text.

Framing Shift

Converting neutral rating scales into satisfaction confirmations.

Negative Suppression

Removing pathways for respondents to report negative experiences.
