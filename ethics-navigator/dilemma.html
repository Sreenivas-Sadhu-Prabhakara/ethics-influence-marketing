The Protagonist

school

Eleanor Carrington

Research Associate — Trend Bridge Market Research Inc.

A newly graduated and ambitious young professional, Carrington entered the corporate research world with strong academic ideals about objectivity, rigor, and ethical conduct. She believed that good research meant honest research.

The Environment

Trend Bridge Market Research Inc. is a fast-paced firm serving mid-sized consumer businesses. In this company, success is not measured by the accuracy of findings—it is measured by how happy the client is with the report.

star Culture

Client satisfaction and supporting client narratives are the primary drivers of success, promotions, and job security.

warning Risk Factor

This culture creates systemic pressure to tailor research findings to match what clients want to hear, rather than what the data actually shows.

bolt

The Trigger Incident

edit_note

Carrington Drafts the Survey

She created well-balanced, unbiased survey questions for a grocery store client, adhering to best practices for neutral research methodology.

swap_horiz

Questions Secretly Altered

Without her knowledge or consent, her carefully crafted questions were modified to guarantee overwhelmingly positive feedback from respondents.

campaign

Discovery at Client Presentation

During the presentation to the grocery store client, Carrington realized the survey she authored was not the survey being presented. The data had been manufactured.

gavel

Supervisor Dismisses Concerns

When she raised the issue, her supervisor was clear: "Pleasing the client takes precedence over strict objectivity."

help

The Central Conflict

Carrington faces a defining career moment. She must choose between three difficult paths—each with profound consequences.

visibility_off

Stay Silent

Accept the status quo. Protect her job, but compromise her integrity.

sync

Conform

Adopt the firm's practices. Advance her career, but perpetuate the cycle.

record_voice_over

Speak Up

Risk her career by challenging the unethical practices she has witnessed.

forum

Discussion Questions

  1. What organizational pressures make it difficult for new employees like Carrington to raise ethical concerns?
  2. Is there a meaningful difference between staying silent and conforming? Why or why not?
  3. How does the power dynamic between a new hire and her supervisor shape the available choices?
  4. If you were in Carrington's position, what would your first step be? Who would you talk to?
  5. Can a company culture that prioritizes client satisfaction ever truly maintain research integrity?
